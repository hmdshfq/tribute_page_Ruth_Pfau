Dr. Ruth Pfau

Pakistan's Mother Teresa

Here is a timeline of Dr. Ruth Pfau's life.

  • 1929 - Born in Leipzig, Germany, to Lutheran Christian parents.
  • 1950s - Studied medicine at the University of Mainz.
  • 1951 - Baptised as an Evangelical Protestant
  • 1953 - Conversion to Roman Catholicism
  • 1957 - Pfau travelled to Paris and joined the Daughters of the Heart of Mary, a Catholic order.
  • 1960 - The order sent her to southern India; however, a visa issue meant she had to stay in Karachi, Pakistan. She travelled to various parts of Pakistan to rescue patients who were abandoned by their families or locked in small rooms for a lifetime.
  • 1960 - Pfau decided to dedicate the rest of her life to the people of Pakistan and their battle against leprosy outbreaks.
  • 1979 - She was appointed as the Federal Advisor on Leprosy to the Ministry of Health and Social Welfare of Government of Pakistan.
  • 1988 - In recognition of her service to the country, she was awarded Pakistani citizenship.
  • 1989 - Pfau received the Hilal-i-Pakistan award presented by the then-President of Pakistan Ghulam Ishaq Khan at the President House for her work with leprosy patients.
  • 1996 - Due to her continued efforts, the World Health Organization declared Pakistan one of the first countries in Asia to have controlled leprosy.
  • 2015 - Pfau was awarded the Staufer Medal, the highest award of the German state of Baden-Württemberg.
  • 2017 - Pfau died at the Aga Khan University Hospital in Karachi after being admitted there due to respiratory problems.

"Dr Ruth Pfau may have been born in Germany, [but] her heart was always in Pakistan. She came here at the dawn of a young nation looking to make lives better for those afflicted by disease and in doing so found herself a home. We will remember her for her courage, her loyalty, her service to the eradication of leprosy and most of all her patriotism."

-- Pakistani Prime Minister Shahid Khaqan Abbasi.
